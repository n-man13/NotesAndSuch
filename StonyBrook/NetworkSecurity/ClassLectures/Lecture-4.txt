вАҐEach party has a pair (K, K-1) of keys : 
вАҐK is the public  key, and used for encryption
вАҐK-1 is the private  key, and used for decryption
вАҐSatisfies    DK-1[EK[M]] = M
вАҐKnowing the public -key K, it is computationally infeasible to compute 
the private key K-1
вАҐHow to check (K,K-1) is a pair?

when P=NP, as deriving K-1 from K is in NP.

available directory

DH and Hard Problems
вАҐDiscrete Log (DLG) Problem : Given <g, h, p>, computes 
a such that ga = h mod p.
вАҐComputational Diffie Hellman (CDH) Problem : Given 
<g, ga mod p,  gb mod p>  (without a, b) compute gab

вАҐDecision Diffie Hellman (DDH) Problem : distinguish

1. If one can solve the DL problem, then one can solve the CDH 
problem.  
2. If one can solve CDH, then one can solve DDH.

Assumptions
вАҐDDH Assumption : DDH is hard to solve .
вАҐCDH Assumption : CDH is hard to solve .
вАҐDLG Assumption :  DLG is hard to solve .
вАҐDDH is assumed difficult to solve for large p in classical computers  
(e.g., at least 1024 bits).
вАҐWarning :
вАҐNew progress in 2013 can solve discrete log for p values with some properties.
